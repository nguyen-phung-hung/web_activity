Issues and solution for Software Ownership Rights & Liabilities

Overview

In the software industry patent trolls are buying broad and vague patents to sue companies for money. These patent trolls do not produce, innovate, or distribute their own products and rely on lawsuits as their main source of income. Oftentimes these patent suits will lose with only about 25% of them winning and the most aggressive patent trolls will only win 10% of the time. Despite these chances of winning, startups and smaller companies will be forced to settle as they simply can’t afford the lawyer fee to defend themselves. This method of earning money decreases innovation, gatekeeps key technology, and slows the industry's growth. Companies instead of investing in their employees and producing products will be forced to slow down their growth to pay for lawsuits. These software patents are especially dangerous because software patents can be vague and broader than patents for physical products.

Solution

In order to solve this problem of patent trolls we can generate awareness of the problem. Like many people, I wasn’t even aware that patent trolls were even a problem so how could people have fought against it? Patent trolls are especially dangerous to startups and smaller companies in the company. Creating awareness and public pressure on these patent trolls can allow for more technologically advanced businesses to stay in the area. I think it would be best to spread awareness from places of education like high schools, community colleges, and universities as there could be potential inventors who would be affected by these patent trolls. We can start by promoting non-profit organizations like the EFF(Electronic Frontier Foundation) that fight directly against patent trolls and lobby Congress to improve patent legislation. They want to impose stricter regulations on the creation of patents so that only truly innovative and unique technologies will be patented.

A second step we can take is to encourage tech companies and new inventors to join the LOT network. This is also a nonprofit organization, but its purpose differs from EFF. They aim to gather groups of companies, schools, and people to join together and create a patent immunization shield that would prevent the possibility of a lawsuit happening in the first place. They do this by giving all their members a lifetime licensing agreement of a patent every time a patent is sold out of the network. This means that even if a patent is sold outside of the network it could not be used against any company that was already in the network, effectively creating a shield.

The third method we can use is supporting or joining the Unified Patents. They are a member-based organization that focuses on challenging bad patents. Their goal is to remove badly made patents all over the world, but their operation is still mainly in the U.S. By challenging the legitimacy of these badly made patents they prevent them from being used maliciously by patent trolls. Their members will also get various benefits like up-to-date information on newly made patents and future litigation suits.

We can support the EFF(Electronic Frontier Foundation) They seem to fight to lower the copyright period. They also seem to be against patents trolls

There was the shield act(The Saving High-Tech Innovators from Egregious Legal Disputes (SHIELD) Act was introduced by Rep. DeFazio, Peter A. [D-OR-4] in 2012 It did not seem to pass because of no support in Congress.
